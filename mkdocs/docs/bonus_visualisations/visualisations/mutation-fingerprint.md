# Mutation Fingerprinting

Mutation Fingerprinting focuses on one selected variant and displays the pattern
of non-synonymous amino-acid changes along the protein sequence.

## What it shows

- the positions of amino-acid changes
- the spread of changes along the protein
- a signature-like overview of what makes the chosen variant distinct

## Why it is useful

This view is helpful for quickly understanding whether a top variant is shaped
by a small number of concentrated changes or by a broader distributed mutation
pattern.
